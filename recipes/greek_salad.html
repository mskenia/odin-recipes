Greek Salad Recipe

Description

Spaghetti Aglio e Olio is a classic Italian pasta dish from Naples, known for its simplicity and bold flavors. Made with just a few pantry staples — garlic, olive oil, chili flakes, and pasta — it’s the perfect example of how minimal ingredients can deliver maximum flavor.

The dish is garlicky, slightly spicy, and rich in olive oil, with a silky texture thanks to the starchy pasta water. It’s quick to make and incredibly satisfying — ideal for a weeknight dinner or a late-night craving.

You can serve it as is, or top it with fresh parsley and grated Parmesan for extra depth and color.

Ingredients

  • 200g (7 oz) spaghetti
  • 4 cloves garlic, thinly sliced
  • 4 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp red chili flakes
  • Salt to taste
  • Fresh parsley (optional)
  • Grated Parmesan (optional)

Steps

  1. Cook the pasta:
    Boil spaghetti in salted water until al dente. Reserve 1/2 cup of the pasta water before draining.
  2. Sauté the garlic:
    In a pan over low heat, add olive oil and sliced garlic. Cook until lightly golden — do not burn!
  3. Add chili flakes:
    Stir in red pepper flakes and cook for a few seconds.
  4. Combine with pasta:
    Add drained pasta to the pan and toss well. Pour in some reserved pasta water to loosen and coat the spaghetti.
  5. Serve:
    Plate immediately. Top with chopped parsley and grated cheese if desired.
